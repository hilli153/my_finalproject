Feline Diseases - Cats

Feline Leukemia Virus:

  • Symptoms: fever, weight loss, anorexia, poor coat condition, enlarged lymph nodes
  • Transmission: due to a severely weakened immune system; typcially suffer from secondary infections
  • Treatment: no cure
  • Prevention: vaccinate and testing

Feline Immunodeficiency Virus:

  • Symptoms: chronic infection of skin, gums, and respiratory system, weight loss, fever, lethargy
  • Transmission: found in saliva and blood; bite wounds; not easily transmitted via contact, sharing food bowl, or grooming
  • Treatment: supportive care, managing secondary infections with antibiotics
  • Prevention: keeping cats indoorsm neutering, testing

Feline Infectious Peritonitis:

  • Symptoms: fever, lethargy, weight loss, neurological issues, swollen belly, inflammation
  • Transmission: stems from feline coronavirus which is shed in feces and saliva; contamination via shared litter box, food bowls, and grooming
  • Treatment: antiviral drugs administered for 84 days, otherwise fatal
  • Prevention: minimizing feline coronavirus exposure, sanitation, and reducing stress

Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease:

  • Symptoms: straining to urinate, frequent and small volume of urine, urinating outside litter box, excessive gential grooming
  • Transmission: not infectious or transmitted between cats; often caused by stress, obesity, or low water intake
  • Treatment: pain medication, dietary changes, surgery
  • Prevention: addressing specific cause - bladder stones, infections, idiopathic cystitis

Feline Herpesvirus:

  • Symptoms: sneezing, thick nasal/eye discharge, conjunctivitis, fever, lethary
  • Transmission: via saliva, nose, or eye secretions through direct contact, shared items, or grooming
  • Treatment: supporitve care including antiviral medication, antibiotics for secondary infections, cleaning
  • Prevention: vaccinate
