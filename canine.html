Canine Diseases - Dogs

Parvovirus:

  • Symptoms: lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea, anorexia, fever
  • Transmission: highly contagious viral disease affecting young and unvaccinated dogs
  • Treatment: agressive therapeutic supportive care, in-hospital or at-home depending on severity
  • Prevention: virus is resistant to common detergents and disinfectants; disinfect/clean with diluted bleach, vaccinate

Kennel Cough/Bordetella:

  • Symptoms: cough, retching/gagging, fever, nasal discharge, depression
  • Transmission: spreads rapidly among susceptible dogs housed in close confinement including clinics, daycare, boarding facilities, and kennels; puppies are more prone
  • Treatment: supportive therapy, isolation, antimicrobial therapy
  • Prevention: vaccinate, isolation if sick

Heartworm:

  • Symptoms: cough, exercise intolerance, difficulty/shorntess of breath, lethargary
  • Transmission: mosquito bite that carries infective larvae, larvae move into heart clogging arteries and lungs as they grow and move
  • Treatment: clinical status dependent, doxycycline, exercise restriction, steroids for trachea issues
  • Prevention: administer preventatives throughout the year

Giardia:

  • Symptoms: diarrhea, weight loss, dehydration, soft feces, rarely vomit
  • Transmission: ingesting cysts from contaminated water, feces, surfaces, or food
  • Treatment: fenbendazole or metronidazole for 3-10 days then re-testing
  • Prevention: feces removal, bathing pets during treatment, cleaning surfaces with ammonium compounds

Distemper:

  • Symptoms: fever, thick eye/nose discharge, cough, vomitting, seizures
  • Transmission: highly contagious, often fatal virus affecting respritatory, digestion, and nervous system
  • Treatment: supportive care case-dependent
  • Prevention: vaccinate
