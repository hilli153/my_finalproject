Zoonotic Diseases - Spread between different species including humans - Human Focus

Rabies:

  • Symptoms: fever, headache, discomfort, confusion, agitation, halucinations, hydrophobia
  • Transmission: 100% fatal viral disease that attacks central nervous system; transmitted via saliva through bites or scratches from infected mammal
  • Treatment: immediate post-exposure vaccine and immunoglobulin required to prevent death
  • Prevention: avoid contact with contaminated animals, seek immediate care, vaccinate pets

Ringworm:

  • Symptoms: itchy, red, scaly, ring-shaped rash
  • Transmission: highly contagious fungal skin infection
  • Treatment: creams, powders, prescrition medication
  • Prevention: keep skin clean and dry, treat infected animals, wash hands after contact

Salmonellosis:

  • Symptoms: fever, diarrhea, cramps
  • Transmission: direct contact with feces of infected animal or indrect contact with their environments
  • Treatment: supportive care, hydration, IV fluids in severe cases
  • Prevention: thorough handwashing after coming into contact; high risk with reptiles and chicks

Leptospirosis:

  • Symptoms: fever, severe headache, muscle aches, vomiting, red eyes, respiratory distress, menigitis
  • Transmission: contact with urine of infected species or contaminated water or soil; entering through skin abrasions, wounds, or mucous membranes
  • Treatment: antibiotic therapy to reduce duration and prevent severe complications; doxycycline or azithromycin; severe cases require hospitalization
  • Prevention: avoid contact with potentially contaminated water, soil, and animal fluids
